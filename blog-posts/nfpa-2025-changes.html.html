Understanding NFPA 2025 Code Changes: What Engineers Need to Know

December 18, 2025 John Smith, PE 8 min read 1,250 views
NFPA Updates

The 2025 edition of NFPA codes brings significant changes that will impact fire protection system design. As engineers, staying ahead of these changes is crucial for compliance and safety.

Major Changes in NFPA 72 (Fire Alarm Systems)

1. Smoke Detector Spacing Updates

The most notable change is in smoke detector spacing requirements. Based on new research:

  • Smooth ceilings under 10 ft: Maximum spacing reduced from 30 ft to 28 ft
  • Ceilings 10-30 ft: New formula: S = 28 - 0.7 × (H - 10)
  • Ceilings over 30 ft: Maximum spacing of 14 ft (previously 15 ft)

2. Voice Intelligibility Requirements

New requirements for voice alarm systems include:

  • Minimum STI (Speech Transmission Index) of 0.50 in all areas
  • Required testing with sound level meters at multiple points
  • Documentation requirements for verification

NFPA 13 (Sprinkler Systems) Updates

1. Extended Coverage Sprinklers

New spacing rules for extended coverage sprinklers:

Hazard Classification Maximum Area (Old) Maximum Area (2025)
Light Hazard 400 ft² 380 ft²
Ordinary Hazard 130 ft² 125 ft²
Extra Hazard 100 ft² 95 ft²

2. K-factor Requirements

Minimum K-factor requirements have been updated based on room size and occupancy.

NFPA 20 (Fire Pumps) Changes

Key updates include:

  1. Jockey Pump Sizing: Minimum capacity increased to 3% of fire pump capacity
  2. Controller Requirements: Enhanced cybersecurity provisions for network-connected controllers
  3. Testing Frequency: Monthly testing now required for all critical facilities
